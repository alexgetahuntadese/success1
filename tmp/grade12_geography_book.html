Ethiopia New Curriculum Grade 12 Geography Student Textbook

The New Curriculum Geography textbook aims to develop students' knowledge, skills, values and attitudes in these core areas of Geography. It uses a thematic approach with a focus on physical geography, human-environment interaction, contemporary issues, and geographic research and mapping. The textbook provides a comprehensive and integrated treatment of crucial geographic topics, equipping students with the knowledge, skills, and perspectives to understand and address the complex issues facing the world today.

Chapters

-

The New Curriculum grade 12 Geography Student Textbook contains 8 units, they are:

  • Unit 1: Major Geological Processes Associated with Plate Tectonics
  • Unit 2: Climate Change
  • Unit 3: Management of Conflict Over Resources
  • Unit 4: Population Policies, Programs and the Environment
  • Unit 5: Challenges of Economic Development
  • Unit 6: Solutions to Environmental and Sustainability Problems
  • Unit 7: Contemporary Global Geographic Issues and Public Concerns
  • Unit 8: Geographical Enquiry and Map Making

Unit 1 - Major Geological Processes Associated with Plate Tectonics

  • Explains the continental drift theory and the evidence supporting it
  • Introduces the plate tectonics theory and the movement of tectonic plates
  • Describes the key types of plate boundaries (convergent, divergent, transform) and associated geological processes

Unit 2 - Climate Change

  • Covers the basic concepts of climate change
  • Examines the trends in global climate change and the natural vs human-induced causes
  • Discusses the consequences of climate change and strategies for adaptation and mitigation
  • Explores Ethiopia's climate resilient green economy initiatives

Unit 3 - Management of Conflict Over Resources

  • Introduces the concept of sustainable development
  • Examines resource use policies and related conflicts
  • Discusses the governance of natural resources
  • Explores indigenous conflict resolution practices

Unit 4 - Population Policies, Programs and the Environment

  • Explores theories on population growth and development
  • Examines population policies and measures to curb population growth
  • Analyzes the relationship between population, socio-economic development, and environmental health

Unit 5 - Challenges of Economic Development

  • Discusses the multiple faces of poverty and its implications for development
  • Examines the advantages and disadvantages of globalization
  • Explores the growing imbalance between regions and countries
  • Addresses the challenge of corruption and global health crises

Unit 6 - Solutions to Environmental and Sustainability Problems

  • Identifies key environmental problems facing the world
  • Discusses the sustainability challenge and the need for solutions
  • Explores the role of environmental education and movements
  • Highlights environmentally friendly indigenous practices

Unit 7 - Contemporary Global Geographic Issues and Public Concerns

  • Focuses on critical issues like climate change, desertification, drought, and famine
  • Examines the public concerns and policy responses to these global geographic challenges

Unit 8 - Geographical Enquiry and Map Making

  • Covers the fundamentals of research in Geography
  • Introduces GIS data and techniques for map making using GIS


Learning Outcome

-

Students will gain understanding in:

  • Understanding the theories of continental drift and plate tectonics, and the geological processes associated with plate movements.
  • Analyzing the causes, trends, consequences, and response strategies related to climate change.
  • Examining the challenges of sustainable development in relation to resource use, population growth, and economic development.
  • Developing skills in geographic research, data collection, and map-making using GIS.
