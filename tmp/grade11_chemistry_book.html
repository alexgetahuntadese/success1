Ethiopia New Curriculum Grade 11 Chemistry Student Textbook

The New Curriculum Grade 11 Chemistry textbook covers the fundamental concepts and principles of atomic structure, chemical bonding, physical states of matter, chemical kinetics, chemical equilibrium, and some important oxygen-containing organic compounds. It aims to reinforce the basic tenets and principles outlined in the Education and Training Policy and provide guidance on active-learning methods and a competency-based approach.

Chapters

-

The New Curriculum grade 11 Chemistry Student Textbook contains 6 units, they are:

  • Unit 1: Atomic Structure And Periodic Properties Of The Elements
  • Unit 2: Chemical Bonding
  • Unit 3: Physical State Of Matter
  • Unit 4: Chemical Kinetics
  • Unit 5: Chemical Equilibrium
  • Unit 6: Some Important Oxygen-containing Organic Compounds

Chapter 1 - Atomic Structure and Periodic Properties of the Elements:

  • Discusses the historical development of atomic theories, including Dalton's atomic theory and the modern atomic theory.
  • Covers early experiments to characterize the atom, such as the discovery of the electron, radioactivity, and the discovery of the neutron.
  • Explains the makeup of the atomic nucleus, electromagnetic radiation, atomic spectra, and the Bohr model of the hydrogen atom.
  • Introduces the quantum mechanical model of the atom, including Heisenberg's uncertainty principle, quantum numbers, and shapes of atomic orbitals.
  • Explores electronic configurations, orbital diagrams, and the periodic table of the elements.

Chapter 2 - Chemical Bonding:

  • Introduces the concept of chemical bonding, including the octet rule and types of chemical bonding (ionic, covalent, and metallic).
  • Explains molecular geometry and intermolecular forces in covalent compounds.
  • Discusses chemical bonding theories such as valence bond theory and molecular orbital theory.
  • Covers the different types of crystal structures.

Chapter 3 - Physical States of Matter:

  • Presents the kinetic theory of matter and the properties of the gaseous, liquid, and solid states.
  • Explains the gas laws and the changes in energy that occur in liquids and solids.

Chapter 4 - Chemical Kinetics:

  • Introduces the concept of the rate of a chemical reaction and the factors affecting the rate of a reaction.

Chapter 5 - Chemical Equilibrium:

  • Discusses the concept of chemical equilibrium, including reversible and irreversible reactions, the equilibrium constant, and Le Chatelier's principle.
  • Explores the applications of equilibrium in industrial processes.

Chapter 6 - Some Important Oxygen-Containing Organic Compounds:

  • Covers the structures, nomenclature, physical properties, and chemical reactions of alcohols, ethers, aldehydes, ketones, carboxylic acids, esters, and fats and oils.

Learning Outcome

-

Students will gain understanding in:

  • The historical development and current models of atomic structure
  • The properties of subatomic particles and their role in atomic structure
  • The principles of quantum mechanics and electronic configurations
  • The periodic properties of elements and their relation to electronic configurations
  • The formation and characteristics of chemical bonds
  • The physical properties of matter in different states and the factors affecting them
  • The concept of chemical kinetics and the factors influencing reaction rates
  • The principles of chemical equilibrium and their applications in industry
  • The structures, properties, and reactions of oxygen-containing organic compounds
