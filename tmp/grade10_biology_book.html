The New Curriculum grade 10 Biology Student Textbook contains 6 units, they are:

Chapter 1: Introduction to Biology

Overview:

This chapter introduces the field of biology, its history, and the scientific method.

Key Topics:

  • Branches of biology
  • Characteristics of living organisms
  • The scientific method
  • Importance of biology

Key Learning Outcomes:

  • Students will understand the scope and significance of biology as a scientific discipline.
  • Students will recognize the key characteristics that define living organisms.
  • Students will describe the steps and principles of the scientific method.
  • Students will appreciate the relevance and applications of biological knowledge in various aspects of life.

Chapter 2: Cell Biology

Overview:

This chapter focuses on the structure and function of cells, including prokaryotic and eukaryotic cells.

Key Topics:

  • Cell theory
  • Cell organelles and their functions
  • Cell membrane and its role
  • Cell division (mitosis and meiosis)
  • The cell cycle

Key Learning Outcomes:

  • Students will understand the basic structure and function of cells.
  • Students will differentiate between prokaryotic and eukaryotic cells.
  • Students will explain the role of various cell organelles and their functions.
  • Students will describe the processes of cell division (mitosis and meiosis) and the cell cycle.
  • Students will recognize the importance of cell biology in the study of living organisms.

Chapter 3: Genetics

Overview:

This chapter explores the principles of genetics, including the structure and function of DNA, inheritance patterns, and genetic variations.

Key Topics:

  • DNA structure and replication
  • Mendelian genetics
  • Genetic disorders
  • Biotechnology and its applications

Key Learning Outcomes:

  • Students will understand the structure and function of DNA as the genetic material.
  • Students will explain the principles of Mendelian genetics and inheritance patterns.
  • Students will identify common genetic disorders and their causes.
  • Students will recognize the applications of biotechnology in various fields.
  • Students will develop an understanding of the role of genetics in the study of living organisms.

Chapter 4: Evolution

Overview:

This chapter focuses on the mechanisms of evolution, the diversity of life, and the principles of ecology.

Key Topics:

  • Theories of evolution
  • Mechanisms of evolution (natural selection, mutation, genetic drift)
  • Speciation and the origin of species
  • Biodiversity and its significance
  • Ecological principles and relationships

Key Learning Outcomes:

  • Students will understand the theories and mechanisms of evolution.
  • Students will explain the process of speciation and the origin of new species.
  • Students will recognize the importance of biodiversity and its conservation.
  • Students will describe the fundamental principles of ecology and the interactions within ecosystems.
  • Students will develop an appreciation for the diversity of life and the evolutionary processes that shape it.

Chapter 5: Human Biology

Overview:

This chapter explores the structure and function of the human body, its systems, and the importance of maintaining good health.

Key Topics:

  • Organ systems in the human body
  • Structure and function of major body systems
  • Homeostasis and its importance
  • Nutrition, exercise, and disease prevention
  • Reproductive system and development

Key Learning Outcomes:

  • Students will understand the structure and function of the major organ systems in the human body.
  • Students will explain the concept of homeostasis and its role in maintaining good health.
  • Students will recognize the importance of a balanced diet, regular exercise, and preventive healthcare.
  • Students will describe the structure and function of the human reproductive system and the process of development.
  • Students will develop an awareness of personal health and the factors that contribute to overall well-being.

Chapter 6: Practical Applications of Biology

Overview:

This chapter focuses on the practical applications of biological knowledge in various fields, such as agriculture, medicine, and biotechnology.

Key Topics:

  • Agriculture and food production
  • Medical applications of biology
  • Biotechnology and its uses
  • Environmental conservation and sustainability

Key Learning Outcomes:

  • Students will understand the role of biology in improving agricultural practices and food production.
  • Students will recognize the contributions of biological research to advancements in medicine and healthcare.
  • Students will explore the applications of biotechnology in various industries and their impact.
  • Students will appreciate the importance of environmental conservation and sustainable practices.
  • Students will develop an understanding of how biological knowledge can be applied to address real-world challenges.
